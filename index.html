ASPIRE Costing Collective

Community of Practice for Cost-Effectiveness Research

Overview

An experimental, voluntary initiative convened by ASPIRE at J-PAL South Asia

The ASPIRE Costing Collective brings together implementation practitioners, donors, and researchers to design and scale cost-effective solutions to complex policy challenges.

Our Guiding Question

How can we do more with every rupee spent on development programs?

Vision

📊 Make cost and value measurement easier to understand, compare, and use when making decisions about scaling effective poverty-reduction interventions.

How We Work

👥
Convene
Bring together a voluntary, cross-sector cohort of practitioners, researchers, funders, and policymakers
🤝
Align
Develop shared understanding of core concepts, frameworks, and tools for cost-effectiveness
💡
Strengthen
Create practical heuristics and methodologies adapted for local contexts
📈
Analyze
Design and pilot data analysis on publicly available cost datasets

Governance Structure

How the Collective is organized and led

Convener: ASPIRE at J-PAL South Asia leads the design of the learning agenda, convenes quarterly meetings, and synthesizes outputs.

Steering Group: Formed by representatives self-nominated to lead each Working Group, rotating bi-annually to ensure diverse leadership.

Working Groups: Meet bi-weekly to review progress and coordinate tasks. Deliver outputs every three months aligned to the quarterly cycle.

WhatsApp Group: All Steering Group and Working Group members coordinate between meetings via a shared WhatsApp Group.
💬 Join WhatsApp Group
